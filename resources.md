# Additional Resources

A short list of resources for going deeper on synthetic biology design. (Not exhaustive — PRs welcome.) This field spans molecular biology, engineering principles, and increasingly computational/ML methods, and moves quickly — cross-check anything specific against current literature before an interview.

## Foundational Reading
- *Genetic Circuit Design Automation* and related foundational synthetic biology engineering literature — good grounding in the engineering-principles framing of the field.
- *Principles of Synthetic Biology* course materials and MOOCs (e.g., from major synthetic biology-focused university programs) for structured foundational learning.
- Cameron, Bashor & Collins, "A brief history of synthetic biology" (or similar field-overview review articles) for grounding in how the field's key concepts and milestones developed.
- Voigt lab and similar groups' publications on genetic circuit design automation and part characterization for a sense of the state of the art in computational design tools (section 5).

## Key Tools & Resources (good to be conversationally familiar with)
- The Registry of Standard Biological Parts and similar characterized-parts repositories, for grounding in the standardization concepts discussed in section 1.
- RBS strength calculators and similar biophysical/ML-based part-performance prediction tools (section 5).
- Genome-scale metabolic models and flux balance analysis software for organisms commonly used as engineering chassis (section 4).
- CRISPR guide RNA design tools that incorporate off-target prediction, for grounding in the genome editing design considerations discussed in section 3.

## Biosafety & Biosecurity Resources
- Guidance from national and international biosafety/biosecurity bodies relevant to your jurisdiction (e.g., national institutes of health biosafety guidelines, WHO guidance on gene drives and dual-use research) — this repo intentionally keeps section 7 at a conceptual level, and anyone working in this space should consult current, authoritative guidance directly.
- Institutional biosafety committee resources and dual-use research of concern policy frameworks at your own or prospective employing institutions.

## Communities & Discussion
- iGEM (International Genetically Engineered Machine) competition resources and team wikis, which document a very wide range of real, documented synthetic biology design projects and their outcomes (including honest accounts of what didn't work).
- r/synbio and r/labrats for broader discussion threads.
- SynBioBeta and similar industry-focused conferences/publications for a sense of current industry application areas and company landscape.

## Companion Repos (adjacent, complementary content)
- **Computational Biologist** — core algorithms, structural/systems biology, statistical methods.
- **Genomics Data Scientist** — NGS pipelines, variant calling, statistical genomics.
- **AI Drug Discovery Scientist** — cheminformatics, generative molecule design, structure-based design.
- **Foundation-Model Scientist (BioAI)** — the ML research methods increasingly applied to genetic part and protein design discussed in section 5.

## Staying Current
- Genome editing tools, DNA synthesis/assembly methods, and computational design tools all evolve quickly — follow recent publications and preprints (bioRxiv) from active synthetic biology research groups and companies.
- Biosecurity screening norms and regulatory frameworks for genetically engineered organisms are actively evolving in multiple jurisdictions — check current guidance directly for anything specific to your work, rather than relying on this repo or older sources.
